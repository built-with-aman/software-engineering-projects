About JIGSAW

Learning shouldn't require thirty open tabs

JIGSAW exists because finding a good learning resource is harder than it should be. Here's what we're building, and why.

Mission

Make the first step of learning obvious

Most students don't fail at learning to code because the material doesn't exist — it's because there's too much of it, unsorted, unranked, and unclear which piece to start with. JIGSAW's mission is to remove that ambiguity: every domain has a clear entry point, every resource is tagged with what it actually teaches and who it's for.

Vision

A learning path for every student, not just the self-directed few

The students who already know how to search well tend to find good resources anyway. JIGSAW is built for everyone else — the fresher who doesn't yet know what a "roadmap" should even contain, and needs one laid out clearly before they can follow it.

Platform Goals

What "done well" looks like for JIGSAW

Coverage

Index quality resources across every major technical domain — not just web development, which most resource lists default to.

Currency

Keep listings current. A resource that was excellent in 2022 but is now outdated should be flagged and replaced, not left to mislead someone in 2026.

Community Trust

Let students who've actually used a resource rate and discuss it — trust built from real experience, not a marketing description.

How It Helps

From "where do I even start" to a finished project

A typical JIGSAW journey moves through four stages — each one solving a different problem students run into on their own.

01

Discover

Browse categorized resources instead of guessing search terms.

02

Follow a Pathway

Move through a sequenced roadmap instead of a random playlist.

03

Ask When Stuck

Post in the community instead of abandoning the resource entirely.

04

Build & Prepare

Finish with real projects and interview preparation, not just video hours.
